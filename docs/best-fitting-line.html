Best fitting line

The purpose of a regression line is to draw a best-fit line through the datapoints. The best fitting line will be a line that is equally distant from the points above and below. In mathematical terms, that line is called the least-squares regression line. Let’s start by understanding what that means.

Let’s take a single variable, Y, being the grade/scores that five students got in my class.

Names=c("Laura", "Peter", "Tom", "Chip","John")
studentN=c(1:5)
Scores=c( 64, 55, 95, 82, 75)
plot(Scores~studentN, xaxt = 'n',xlab="Student name")
axis(1, at=1:5, labels=Names)

What will be the best-fit line for that single variable?. Think about you, you should know it. This about a line that will equally space the points below and above?.

I hope you said the “mean”. That is correct, in the case of a single variable the “best-fitting line” is the line drawn by the mean.

Let’s verify: In the figure above, the red-line is the mean. How far is each point from the mean is indicated with the orange vertical lines, and in the case of the regression model, that difference between the given point and the best-fitting line is called a residual. It may also be named residual errors. Why?. well in the case of the linear regression, we want a model that best describes the data. Unfortunately, the best-line does not pass by every single one of the points, so that difference between the each point and the line defined by the model is the error of the model.

For it to be the best-fitting line, the residual errors have to be the same above an below the line. Lets check.

Residuals below the line: \((-10.2)^2 + (-19.2)^2 =472.68\)

Residuals above the line: \((20.8)^2 + (7.8)^2 + (0.8)^2\)
